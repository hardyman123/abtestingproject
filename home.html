A/B Testing

A/B Testing

This project revolved around making two versions of a cactus shopping website and collecting data regarding two main metrics:
    - Completion time to add $150 worth of cacti to the cart
    - Return rate of going back to the home page after cart

These actions were measured and then underwent statistical analysis to determine whether or not the changes from each site played a part in changing how users interacted with the site

The site in question: https://obscure-tundra-08863.herokuapp.com/

Time to Completion

Null Hypothesis: both sites will take the same amount of time to complete a task

Alternate Hypothesis: Site B takes shorter time to complete tasks
(The reason being the add to cart button is placed in a position that makes more sense in terms of information consumption flow)

Return Rate

Null Hypothesis: Users return to the homepage an equal amount of times

Alternate Hypothesis: Site B encourages users to return to the site a greater amount of times
(The reason being that the button placement and general centering of the title may make the site seem more professional)

Data Collection

My data collection process was very simple in terms of getting it. I got the majority of my data from the people in my studio section who clicked on my link. As for the rest, I asked friends and went into groupchats and asked people to fulfill the criteria based on what we needed ($150 of cacti in cart). Because I asked for a specific action this may have an impact on the data's reliability.
